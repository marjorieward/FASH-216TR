Before Class:

  • Bring the following pattern pieces (cut, notched, and marks transferred) to class:
    • Sleeve Sample (1 piece)
    • Sleeve Sample Placket (2 pieces)
    • Sleeve Sample Cuff (2 pieces)
  • Bring the following materials to class:
    • Half Scale Oaktag Slopers
    • 0.5 or 0.7 mm mechanical pencils
    • Erasers (if needed)
    • Gridded or alphanumeric pattern paper
    • Paper Scissors
    • ½ inch wide MATTE/SATIN FINISH scotch tape
    • Clear straight ruler with 1/8 inch markings
    • Pushpins or sewing pins
    • Fashion French curve/Varyform curve
    • Half Scale L-Square ruler (optional)
    • Medium weight muslin
    • Sewing Supplies - See syllabus for necessary sewing materials

During Class:

  • Separate button placket
  • Add-on/folded button placket
  • Hidden button placket
  • Sewing the sleeve placket sample.

After Class:

  • Complete the basic shirt/blouse with sleeve and all 3 button placket drafting samples in half scale and bring them to our next class meeting.
  • Carefully cut and sew the sleeve placket sewing sample in full scale and bring it to our next class meeting.
  • Continue work on the Modular Dress project.
    • Choose one draft exercise or design illustration from pp.245-247, 250-259, 263-264, 267-275, 285, 300 (hardcover textbook); pp.289-291, 294-303, 307-308, 311-319, 329, 344 (softcover textbook).
    • Modify your Full-Scale front and back skirt slopers to suit your chosen draft/illustration.
    • Transform your full-scale drafts in patterns by adding seam allowance (SA), hem allowance (HA), notches, tailor's tacks, and requested pattern notation.
    • Carefully cut and sew your final bodice patterns in muslin or fashion fabric.
    • Attach your skirt to the Bodice from Week Two.
    • See the Modular Dress assignment brief for details.
